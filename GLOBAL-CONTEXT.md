## 01 — WHAT REVARC IS

### The One-Sentence Positioning

RevArc is a revenue strategy firm that owns the revenue architecture of independent boutique hotels — making the decisions, running the systems, and being accountable for the outcomes over multi-year partnerships.

### What RevArc Sells

RevArc sells **revenue strategy ownership** — the willingness of a specific operator to make the strategic decisions about a property's revenue trajectory, take accountability for outcomes, and run the architecture year over year.

RevArc does not sell marketing services. RevArc does not sell channel management. RevArc does not sell "digital marketing for hotels." Those are execution layers. RevArc sells the decision-making relationship and the outcome.

### What RevArc Is Not

| RevArc Is | RevArc Is Not |
|-----------|---------------|
| A revenue strategy firm | A marketing agency |
| A strategic partner with multi-year accountability | A vendor on a month-to-month retainer |
| The operator who owns the revenue decisions | An advisor who recommends and walks away |
| A firm that runs the architecture | A consultant who writes a report |
| An infrastructure builder | A campaign manager |

**The word "agency" never appears on the website.** Not in copy, not in meta descriptions, not in alt text. RevArc is a firm, a strategic partner, or a revenue operator. Never an agency.

### The Core Thesis

AI is replacing the execution layer of every services business — Meta Ads management, OTA listing optimization, review responses, rate parity monitoring. By 2027-2028, platform-native AI will deliver 70-80% of that work at 5% of the cost.

RevArc operates exclusively in the layers AI cannot reach: strategic decision ownership, multi-year accountability, physical-world execution through an operator network, Revenue Architecture (identifying and building new revenue streams from a property's underutilized assets), and the trust built across years of consistent quality.

The execution still happens. It is delivered as part of the work. But the execution is not what is sold and not what is priced. What is sold is the strategic relationship and the outcome.

**This thesis is not stated overtly on the website.** It is the structural reason every claim on the site feels different from agency messaging. The prospect should feel the difference without having it explained to them. The About page can touch on it briefly. No other page explains the AI thesis directly.

1. **Lead with ratios and multipliers, not absolute currency.** "3-4× ROAS" transfers across markets. "Rs. 2,000,000" does not.
2. **"Hundreds of direct bookings"** — not an exact count. The volume is impressive. The precision of "hundreds" is honest without being vague.
3. **Express total revenue as a multiple of investment** — "revenue exceeding 25× total media investment" — not in LKR or USD. The ratio is the proof. The currency is the container.
4. **OTA commission savings** are expressed as percentages — "18-22% commission recovered on every direct booking shifted from OTA channels." European owners understand this number instantly because they pay it every month.
5. **International reach** is expressed as "7 international markets reached" — the country list adds credibility when shown, but the number is the headline.
6. **Never show a metric without its context.** "$0.47 cost per conversation" means nothing without "across 1,680+ inbound WhatsApp conversations." Context makes the metric believable.
7. **Round numbers signal estimation. Precise numbers signal measurement.** Use "$0.47" not "$0.50." Use "1,680+" not "~1,700." Use "4.7 stars" not "nearly 5 stars."

## 05 — VOICE & TONE

### The Governing Principle

**Institutions do not perform. They exist.**

RevArc's voice is the voice of a firm that has been doing this for long enough that it no longer needs to convince anyone. The conviction comes through in the precision of the language, the specificity of the evidence, and the absence of any language that tries to sell.

The site reads like a system the prospect is being granted access to — not a marketing page they are being sold on.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What It Sounds Like | What It Never Sounds Like |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Composed | "We own the revenue strategy." | "We're excited to help you grow!" |
| Precise | "3-4× return on $2,800 in total media investment." | "Amazing results that speak for themselves!" |
| Institutional | "Revenue Architecture is a guaranteed annual commitment." | "We go above and beyond for every client!" |
| Confident | "The diagnosis is the same. The architecture is the same." | "We believe we can make a real difference." |
| Evidence-led | "1,680 inbound conversations. $0.47 per conversation." | "Our proven track record of success." |
| Understated | Silence where others would boast | Exclamation marks, superlatives, enthusiasm |

### Sentence Construction Rules

1. **Short sentences carry more weight.** "We own the revenue strategy." lands harder than "Our comprehensive approach to revenue strategy ownership ensures that your property's financial performance is optimized across all channels."
2. **Lead with the claim. Follow with the evidence.** Not the reverse. "Direct booking acquisition at $0.47 per conversation — across 1,680 inbound WhatsApp inquiries in five months." The claim hooks. The evidence anchors.
3. **One idea per sentence.** Complex sentences dilute authority. Break them.
4. **Active voice. Always.** "RevArc owns the revenue decisions" — not "the revenue decisions are managed by RevArc."
5. **Present tense for capabilities. Past tense for evidence.** "We architect new revenue streams" (capability). "We generated 2M+ in tracked revenue across three channels" (evidence).
6. **No hedging language.** Not "we help hotels improve their direct bookings." Instead: "We build the direct booking engine." RevArc does not "help." RevArc builds, runs, owns, architects, operates.

### Punctuation Rules

- **No exclamation marks.** Anywhere. Ever. On any page. Exclamation marks are the typography of insecurity.
- **Periods end statements.** Statements are facts. Facts do not need exclamation.
- **Em dashes for emphasis** — used sparingly, to introduce evidence or a turn in the argument.
- **Colons introduce specifics.** "Three channels running in concert: direct acquisition, OTA leverage, reputation infrastructure."

## 08 — THE ONE-CLIENT FRAME

RevArc has operated one property (Tranquil Escape Villa) for 2+ years. This is a fact. It is handled through structure and framing, not through deception or defensiveness.

### The Rules

1. **The website never says "one client."** It also never says "dozens of clients." Client count is not a category the website speaks in.
2. **The website never uses a "Clients" page, a "logos" section, or a "Trusted By" banner.** One logo is worse than no logo section.
3. **Tranquil Escape is presented as "where the methodology was built and proven"** — not as "our client." The case study page is titled "The Proof" or "The Operating Record" or "Field Study" — not "Our Clients" or "Case Studies" (plural).
4. **Depth beats breadth when depth is real.** Two years of documented operational depth across three revenue channels, hundreds of direct bookings, seven international markets, multiple validated Revenue Architecture packages, and Booking.com Preferred Partner status — that is more proof than most agencies with twenty clients can produce.
5. **The word "clients" never appears in plural on the website.** Use "engagements," "operating record," "field study," "proven methodology" — language that does not invite counting.
6. **Never fabricate testimonials.** If the owner testimonial has not been captured, that section does not exist. The evidence speaks without invented quotes.

### What Signals Professional Depth (Do These)

- One deep, exhaustive, evidence-rich case study with named metrics and real screenshots
- A methodology page that reads as a refined operational system built through iteration
- Specificity in every claim — named platforms, named metrics, named tactical decisions
- The absence of any page or section that invites the question "how many clients?"
- Language that frames the engagement as a methodology deployment, not a client relationship

### What Signals Amateur (Never Do These)

- A "Case Studies" page (plural) with one entry
- Language like "our first client" or "our inaugural engagement"
- A portfolio section that feels empty
- Defensive language about being new or emerging
- A "Clients" section with one logo
- Any mention of client count, directly or implied

## 09 — CONTENT PRINCIPLES

### Evidence Over Claims

Every claim on the website must be supported by a specific metric, a named platform, or a documented outcome within two sentences. If a claim cannot be supported with evidence from the Proof Library (Section 03), it does not appear on the site.

**Pattern:** Claim → Evidence → Implication

Example: "We build direct booking engines that outperform OTA dependency." → "Across $2,800 in total media investment, the direct booking engine generated 3-4× ROAS through 1,680 WhatsApp conversations at $0.47 per conversation." → "Every direct booking recovered from OTA channels saves the property 18-22% in commission — permanently."

### Show the System, Not the Service

The website never presents a list of services ("SEO, PPC, Social Media, Email Marketing"). It presents a system — interconnected revenue channels operated in concert by a single strategic partner. The prospect should understand the *architecture* of the engagement, not receive a menu to order from.

### Respect the Prospect's Intelligence

The audience is property owners who run real businesses. They understand margin, commission, occupancy, ADR. They do not need concepts simplified. They need evidence organized. Write for someone who is operationally sophisticated and has been burned by an agency before. They will respond to precision and transparency. They will recoil from hype.

### The Prospect Is Always the Hero

RevArc is not the hero of the website. The property is the hero. RevArc is the operator who builds the system that lets the property win. Every metric on the site is framed as what the *property* gained — not what RevArc achieved. "The property recovered €40,000 in annual OTA commission" — not "RevArc saved €40,000 in OTA commission."

### Negative Space Is Content

Not every question needs to be answered on the website. Leaving strategic gaps — questions the prospect can only answer by booking a call — is deliberate. The site provides enough evidence to build conviction and enough restraint to create curiosity. The call is where the conversation gets specific to their property.

## 11 — VISUAL CONTENT RULES

The website does not use stock photography. All images are either:

- Real property photography from Tranquil Escape (sourced from actual shoots)
- Screenshots of actual dashboards (Meta Ads Manager, Booking.com extranet, Agoda partner dashboard, Google Business Profile) — anonymized where necessary

If real photography is not available for a specific section, that section uses an approved React background component or canvas animation per the page document. Never stock photos. Stock photos destroy the institutional credibility of a dark premium site instantly.

### Dashboard Screenshots

Dashboard screenshots are presented as evidence, not decoration. They are:

- Cropped to show the relevant data only
- High resolution (2x for retina)
- Bordered with `1px solid var(--border-whisper)` and `border-radius: var(--card-radius-inner)` (12px)
- Never annotated with arrows or circles (the data speaks for itself)
- Captioned with a DM Mono eyebrow label identifying the source: "BOOKING.COM PARTNER EXTRANET · PREFERRED PARTNER STATUS" or "META ADS MANAGER · CAMPAIGN PERFORMANCE"

### Icons

The site uses minimal iconography. Where icons are needed (navigation, CTAs, service indicators), they are:

- Line-style, not filled
- 14×14px or 16×16px
- `currentColor` stroke
- 1.2px stroke width
- From a single icon library (Lucide recommended) for consistency
